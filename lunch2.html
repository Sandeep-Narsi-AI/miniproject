Lunch - Vegitable Biryani

Ingredients Rice & Veggies The Biryani

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup basmati rice
  2. 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, potatoes, etc.), chopped
  3. 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  4. 1 large tomato, chopped
  5. 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  6. 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  7. 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  8. 2 tablespoons biryani masala
  9. 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  10. 2-3 green chilies, slit
  11. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  12. 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  13. 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  14. 4-5 whole cloves
  15. 4-5 whole green cardamom pods
  16. 1-2 bay leaves
  17. 1-inch cinnamon stick
  18. 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  19. Salt to taste
  20. 2 cups water (for cooking rice)

Rice & Veggies

Preparing Rice:

  1. Rinse basmati rice under running water until the water runs clear.
  2. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes.
  3. In a large pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil.
  4. Add soaked and drained rice, a pinch of salt, and cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked.
  5. Drain the rice and set it aside.

Cooking Vegitables:

  1. In a separate pan, heat ghee or oil over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds, whole cloves, green cardamom pods, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick. Sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  6. Add mixed vegetables, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.
  7. Stir in biryani masala, chopped mint leaves, and chopped coriander leaves.

The Biryani

Layering biryani:

  1. In a large, heavy-bottomed pan or biryani pot, start layering. Begin with a layer of partially cooked rice.
  2. Follow with a layer of the vegetable mixture.
  3. Drizzle some plain yogurt over the vegetable layer.
  4. Repeat the layers until all the rice and vegetables are used, finishing with a layer of rice on top.
  5. Sprinkle some biryani masala and chopped mint leaves over the top layer.

Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):

  1. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with aluminum foil to trap the steam.
  2. Place the pot on a very low flame and let it cook on dum for 20-25 minutes. Alternatively, you can place the pot in a preheated oven at 350°F (180°C) for the same duration.
  3. Allow the biryani to rest for a few minutes before gently fluffing the rice with a fork.

!!!Enjoy your delicious homemade vegetable biryani!!!
