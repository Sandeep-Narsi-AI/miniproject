Lunch - Sambar

Ingredients Before Sambar Making Sambar

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  2. 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, pumpkin, drumsticks, etc.)
  3. 1 small onion, chopped
  4. 1 medium-sized tomato, chopped
  5. Tamarind pulp (lemon-sized ball soaked in water)
  6. 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  7. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  8. 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  9. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  10. A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  11. 2-3 dried red chilies
  12. A few curry leaves
  13. 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  14. Salt to taste
  15. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Before Sambar

Cooking Toor Dal:

  1. Rinse toor dal under running water until the water runs clear.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add the rinsed toor dal, chopped onion, chopped tomato, mixed vegetables, turmeric powder, and enough water.
  3. Pressure cook until the dal is soft and the vegetables are cooked. Set aside.

Preparing Tamarind Pulp:

  1. Soak a lemon-sized ball of tamarind in warm water for about 15 minutes.
  2. Squeeze the tamarind to extract the pulp, and discard the solids. Set aside.

Making Sambar

  1. In a large pot or kadai, heat oil or ghee over medium heat.
  2. Add mustard seeds; when they splutter, add cumin seeds, dried red chilies, and asafoetida.
  3. Add curry leaves and chopped onions. Sauté until the onions become translucent.
  4. Add sambar powder and sauté for a minute to release the flavors.
  5. Pour the tamarind pulp into the pot and bring it to a boil.
  6. Add the cooked toor dal and vegetable mixture. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed.
  7. Add salt to taste and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Tadka (Tempering):

  1. In a small pan, heat a teaspoon of ghee or oil.
  2. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried red chilies, and curry leaves.
  3. Once the mustard seeds splutter, pour this tempering over the sambar.

Final Touch:

  1. Mix well and let the sambar simmer for a few more minutes.
  2. Check for seasoning and adjust if necessary.
  3. Serve hot sambar with steamed rice, idli, dosa, or vada.

!!!Enjoy your homemade delicious and nutritious sambar!!!
